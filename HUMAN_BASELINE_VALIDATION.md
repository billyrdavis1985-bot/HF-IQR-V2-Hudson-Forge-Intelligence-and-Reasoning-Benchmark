## Overview

The researcher completed a 20-question subset
(10% stratified sample) from the full benchmark
as an independent human baseline.

## Results

| Metric | Result | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Accuracy | 56.25% (9/16 fully correct, 1 partial) | Baseline difficulty confirmed |
| Trap Detection Rate | 100% (20/20 traps identified) | Design validation confirmed |
| Average Time per Question | 8-15 minutes | Cognitive load confirmed |

## Key Finding

Trap detection did not correlate with solution
correctness. The researcher identified 100% of
embedded traps but only solved 56.25% of questions
correctly. This suggests the benchmark tests
reasoning capacity beyond pattern recognition.

## Implication

The five-round deliberation protocol is not
artificial overhead but represents necessary
engagement with genuinely difficult material.
Frontier models achieving 88-98% accuracy on
these questions suggests their advantage comes
from structured reasoning processes not inherent
problem-solving superiority.
